Unmanned Surface Vehicles (USV) (Updating)

Unmanned surface vehicle – USV, is used for many military and civilian purposes such as mapping, finding and rescue, finding and rescuing, water environment monitoring and even for transport or traveling. However, compared with other unmanned vehicles such as UAV or UGV, motions controlling for USV is more sophisticated, because of impacts from wind, water current and the vehicle's inertia. Consequently, planning a possible path for USVs' movements to complete assigned tasks with high efficiency and saving energy is essential.

In this blog, we proceed to develop an USV system based on the available VIAM- USV2000 system, and based on TSP problem and the Dubins curve theory to plan the trajectory through multiple reference points on the map, and directs the USV to follow a predetermined trajectory.
